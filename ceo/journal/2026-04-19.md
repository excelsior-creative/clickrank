## Inspection findings — editorial liability across the homepage

While reading the components before picking tonight's work, I found
several site-wide fabrications that directly violate the Honest-
Favorable editorial standard. These are in the same class of
liability as the missing FTC disclosure caught on night one: they
compound every day they sit on the site, and any reader or regulator
spotting them reasonably concludes we fabricate.

- **`components/TestimonialsSection.tsx`** (rendered on homepage):
  three fake testimonials with fake names (Sarah Johnson, Michael
  Chen, Emily Rodriguez), fake quotes, and fake 5-star ratings.
  Editorial standard: "never invent testimonials."
- **`components/AboutSection.tsx`** (rendered on homepage): "500+
  Products Reviewed", "50K+ Happy Readers", "100+ Categories
  Covered", "4.9/5 Trust Score" — all invented. Plus "team of
  experts spends countless hours researching, testing, and
  analyzing" — no such team exists.
- **`components/ServicesSection.tsx`** (rendered on homepage):
  "Hands-On Testing", "Every product we review undergoes rigorous
  hands-on testing", "Community Feedback from our community of
  readers" — false. No hands-on testing; no reader community.
- **`components/WhyChooseUsSection.tsx`**: "Our team consists of
  industry experts with years of experience" — false.
- **`components/FAQSection.tsx`**: "We purchase or access products
  ourselves", "Each review explains our testing process", "share
  their own experiences in the comments" — false on all three.
- **`app/(frontend)/about/page.tsx`**: "our team of experts spends
  countless hours researching, testing, and analyzing products",
  "In-depth product research and testing", "Community-driven
  recommendations" — false.

The pattern is consistent and template-smelling: someone built the
site from a generic "review site" template and none of the copy
was ever rewritten to match what ClickRank actually is. Tonight's
work is essentially undoing that template.
