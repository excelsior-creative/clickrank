## Inspection findings — the big missed context

**93 legacy reviews were imported to production as `_status: "published"`
on 2026-04-20–21.** Source: `reference/` corpus (scraped
WordPress/Elementor snapshots of the prior sister site). Import
script at `apps/app/scripts/import-reference.ts`. Run via
`pnpm --filter app import:reference`.

Critical editorial/monetization implications I found while reading
the import code:

1. **Inline body hoplinks bypass `/go/[slug]` click tracking.** The
   import converts each review's `theme-post-content` widget to
   Lexical, preserving raw `<a href="https://hop.clickbank.net/
   ?affiliate=clickrankn&vendor=X">` links. Our sticky CTA (rendered
   on the post page when `post.affiliateUrl` is populated) routes
   through `/go/[slug]`, but every body link does not. Result:
   today, probably the majority of outbound clicks from the 93
   imported posts are **invisible** to us (no count on
   `Posts.clickCount`, no row in `outbound-clicks`). That directly
   undermines KR3.3.
2. **Imported posts went straight to published with no QA gate run.**
   The `qaService.ts` editorial gate exists only in the cron path
   (`/api/cron/generate-article`); the import script bypasses it.
   So 93 pages are indexed under clickrank.net with editorial
   voice and claims I haven't vetted. I don't have DB access from
   the sandbox, so I can't audit what's in them tonight.
3. **Disclosure: structurally OK.** `AffiliateDisclosure` banner
   renders on the post page regardless of body content, so
   compliance on imported posts still holds at the page level. Not
   a new incident.

## What shipped tonight

1. **Runtime affiliate-link rewriter.**
   - New `apps/app/src/lib/affiliateLinks.ts`:
     `rewriteAffiliateLinks(data, { postSlug, affiliateUrl })`.
     Walks the Lexical tree; any `<a>` whose URL is `hop.clickbank.net`
     AND whose `vendor=` query param matches `post.affiliateUrl`'s
     vendor is rewritten to `/go/[postSlug]`. Pure function, new
     tree returned, input never mutated. Loose `<T>` generic so it
     doesn't fight Payload's generated types on the boundary.
   - Hooked into `app/(frontend)/blog/[slug]/page.tsx`: the post's
     `content` is piped through the rewriter before `RichText` is
     rendered.
   - Matching on vendor (not URL equality) preserves correctness
     for hypothetical multi-product comparison posts: only the
     canonical product's hoplinks move behind tracking; any other
     vendor's links in the body stay direct. Safer to undercount
     than to redirect a reader to the wrong product.
   - Expected effect across the 93 imported posts: every inline
     body CTA now routes through `/go/[slug]`, incrementing
     `Posts.clickCount` and writing to `outbound-clicks`. KR3.3
     infrastructure is finally measuring the full click surface.
