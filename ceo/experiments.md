# Experiments

## How to use this file

For each experiment:
- **Hypothesis** — what do we believe?
- **Test** — how will we check it?
- **Success criteria** — what result proves/disproves?
- **Result** — what actually happened, with date.

### EXP-001 — Does a top-of-post FTC disclosure banner hurt CTR?

- **Hypothesis:** A compact, polite disclosure banner above the
  title *does not* measurably hurt outbound affiliate CTR vs. a
  footer-only disclosure. It should improve trust.
- **Test:** After click tracking lands, A/B: group A gets banner,
  group B gets footer disclosure only.
- **Success criteria:** Banner variant's outbound CTR is within
  10% of footer-only variant; net conversion unchanged or better.
- **Result:** Pending click tracking.
- **Note:** Even if CTR were to drop meaningfully, we'd keep the
  banner — it's a legal/editorial requirement. The experiment
  is to quantify the cost, not decide whether to run it.

### EXP-002 — Does adding "What could be better" reduce CTR?

- **Hypothesis:** Adding an honest 1–2 item caveats section
  improves trust and conversion relative to zero-caveat reviews.
- **Test:** A/B two versions of the same review once we have
  traffic.
- **Success criteria:** Caveat version wins on bounce rate and
  outbound CTR.
- **Result:** Pending.

### EXP-003 — Does a mid-post "Verdict" callout beat an end-of-post CTA?

- **Hypothesis:** A styled "Our verdict" box with a CTA placed at
  ~60% scroll depth earns more outbound clicks than a CTA only
  at the end.
- **Test:** A/B after click tracking.
- **Success criteria:** Mid-post variant beats end-of-post by
  ≥20% CTR.
- **Result:** Pending.
