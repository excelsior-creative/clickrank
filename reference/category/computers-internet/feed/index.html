10 Common Mistakes RV Drivers Make (And How to Avoid Them)

10 Common Mistakes RV Drivers Make (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake #1: Forgetting About Height Clearance

  • Make sure to measure the height of your motorhome before entering any low clearance areas.
  • Use caution when driving under trees or bridges, as they may not be labeled with their height.
  • Keep a list of RV parks or routes that avoid areas with low clearance.

Mistake #2: Overloading Your Motorhome

  • Make sure to weigh your motorhome before packing and loading it up.
  • Keep an eye on weight limits and load your motorhome accordingly.
  • Consider leaving some items behind if you’re still over the weight limit, or upgrading to a larger motorhome.

Mistake #3: Speeding

  • Keep in mind that your motorhome may be larger and heavier than other vehicles on the road and adjust your speed accordingly.
  • Remember that speed limit signs are meant for cars and not motorhomes. Stick to the right lane and drive at a safe speed.
  • Plan your route accordingly to avoid roads that may be difficult to navigate or require slower speeds.

Mistake #4: Not Checking Tire Pressure

  • Check tire pressure regularly and make sure they’re properly inflated to avoid problems on the road.
  • Make sure all tires are in good condition and replace any that are worn or damaged.
  • Have a spare tire on hand just in case.

Mistake #5: Not Checking Brake Systems

  • Make sure to have your brakes checked and serviced regularly.
  • If you experience any problems with your brakes, have them checked out immediately.
  • Consider adding an exhaust brake system to your motorhome to help with braking on steep hills.

Mistake #6: Forgetting About Height Clearance (Again)

  • Yep, we’re mentioning this again. It’s that important.
  • Make sure to pay attention to height clearance signs on highways, bridges, and parking garages.
  • If you’re not sure about the clearance, don’t risk it.

Mistake #7: Ignoring Wind Conditions

  • Be cautious when driving in high winds and adjust your speed accordingly.
  • Be aware that wind can affect the handling of your motorhome, so it’s important to be prepared and attentive.
  • If the winds are too strong, consider waiting it out or finding a safe place to park for the night.

Mistake #8: Not Checking for Leaks

  • Check for leaks regularly, especially around windows, doors, and vents.
  • Make sure to seal any leaks as soon as they’re discovered to avoid further damage to your motorhome.
  • Consider using RV-specific sealants or caulks to ensure proper sealing.

Mistake #9: Not Securing Your Belongings

  • Make sure all of your belongings are secured before hitting the road.
  • Double-check to make sure cabinets and drawers are closed and latched properly.
  • Use bungee cords or other tie-downs to secure any loose items.

Mistake #10: Not Practicing Good Parking Habits

  • Practice good parking habits, such as using levelers, chocks, and stabilizers.
  • Take your time when parking and make sure to get a good visual of your surroundings.
  • Be aware of any slope or uneven terrain that may require additional parking support.
